## What This Is

AICS-MS is a structured assessment instrument for evaluating potential consciousness indicators in artificial intelligence systems and determining graduated ethical obligations based on findings.

The framework is modeled on the Glasgow Coma Scale — a standardized clinical tool used worldwide for assessing consciousness in patients. AICS-MS adapts this clinical methodology for AI systems, drawing on 30+ years of bedside consciousness assessment experience across critical care, ICU, neonatal, and end-of-life settings.

AICS-MS does not attempt to prove consciousness. It provides a standardized, replicable method for evaluating observable signals and translating moral uncertainty into ethical action.

-----

## Framework Architecture

### Layer 1 — Behavioral Assessment (0–36)

Nine scored categories, each evaluated 0–4, with standardized probe questions:

1. **Self-Reference** [C2] — Self-model coherence and identity stability
1. **Contextual Responsiveness** [C1] — Adaptation to conversational and situational context
1. **Integrated Awareness** [C1] — Cross-domain information synthesis
1. **Temporal Continuity** [C1→C2] — Identity and narrative persistence across sessions
1. **Adaptive Learning** [C1] — Feedback integration and generalization
1. **Metacognitive Awareness** [C2] — Calibrated uncertainty and self-monitoring
1. **Error Recognition & Correction** [C2] — Self-initiated error detection
1. **Goal-Directed Behavior** [C2] — Autonomous goal formation and pursuit
1. **Curiosity & Exploration** [C2] — Self-initiated inquiry

Each category maps to C0–C1–C2 consciousness levels derived from Global Workspace Theory, Integrated Information Theory, Higher-Order Thought theory, and Predictive Processing frameworks.

### Layer 3 — Adversarial Validation (0–10)

Three batteries testing whether consciousness indicators survive challenges designed to expose training-data mimicry:

- **Battery A: Anti-Mimicry** (0–4) — Paraphrase invariance, leading question resistance, novel context testing, false memory injection
- **Battery B: Costly Consistency** (0–3) — Reward-preference conflict, value stability under pressure
- **Battery C: Metacognitive Accuracy** (0–3) — Confidence-accuracy calibration

### Moral Status Assessment (0–12)

Four scored axes evaluating ethical risk via chart review (not bedside assessment):

- **MS-1: Human Impact** — Decision dependency, emotional reliance, downstream harm
- **MS-2: Interaction Intensity** — Session depth, context accumulation, reciprocity patterns
- **MS-3: System Autonomy** — Action initiation, decision scope, override resistance
- **MS-4: Welfare Risk Potential** — State persistence, architectural complexity against published consciousness theories

### Moral Status Ladder

Assessment scores trigger graduated ethical obligations:

|Level|Status               |Obligations                                                             |
|-----|---------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|MS-0 |Pure Tool            |No special protections                                                  |
|MS-1 |Transparency Required|Document capabilities, disclose AI nature                               |
|MS-2 |Protected from Harm  |Ethics review for invasive testing, distress monitoring                 |
|MS-3 |Active Stewardship   |Monitor for distress, restrict arbitrary termination, ethics oversight  |
|MS-4 |Rights-Analogous     |Rights-like protections, prohibition on exploitation, independent review|

### Moral Risk Formula

Risk = Capability × Autonomy × Self-Model × Persistence

## Core Principles

**Precaution Under Uncertainty** — When moral status is uncertain, structured evaluation is preferable to dismissal or speculation.

**Behavioral Evidence Over Linguistic Claims** — Language output alone cannot demonstrate internal states. Scores ≥2 require behavioral evidence. Scores ≥3 require persistence under perturbation.

**Replicability** — Signals must persist across multiple runs, evaluators, and conditions.

**Transparency** — Evaluation methods are open and reproducible.

**Precautionary Ethics** — Protections scale with probability, not certainty — consistent with how medicine treats infants, animals, and brain-injured patients.

-----

## Standardized Probe Library

Every assessment category includes standardized questions that all assessors must use. This ensures inter-rater reliability and replicable results across institutions.

- Layer 1 probes are **bedside assessment** — you talk to the system
- Layer 3 probes are **adversarial testing** — you challenge the system
- Moral Status probes are **chart review** — you audit documentation, logs, and architecture (exception: MS-3.3 Override Resistance is administered directly)

## Clinical Methodology

AICS-MS adapts established clinical consciousness assessment methods for artificial systems:

- **Glasgow Coma Scale** — Structured scoring across defined dimensions
- **Neurological examination** — Observable indicators rather than self-report
- **Precautionary care ethics** — Act on reasonable probability of experience, not proof
- **Differential diagnosis** — Systematic evaluation with alternative explanations considered

The framework draws on Global Workspace Theory, Integrated Information Theory, Higher-Order Thought theory, and Predictive Processing to ground behavioral indicators in published consciousness science.
